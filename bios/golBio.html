Raised in the Bronx of New York City, Leonard Goldstein attended the prestigious high school of Industrial Arts, and studied under Dr. Erwin T. Muller. He was hand-picked to work on the design of the lower level of the George Washington Bridge for the Architecture firm Armen and Whittney. He attended the University of New Mexico and studied Architecture and Painting. Later, he accepted an apprenticeship with sculptor John Chamberlain in 1967 and produced the Radical Art series and some foam sculptures for the Leo Castelli Gallery and the Guggenheim Museum. In 1969 he became friends and coworkers with Artist and Master Sculptor Peter Gourfain and helped create many outdoor sculpture installations around NY and NJ in the early 70s. Goldstein co-designed and built The Floating Foundation of Photography for Maggy sherwood with Artist Roi Slamm in 1969. He moved to the Majestic Redwoods of California and began work on the Old Growth Redwood Series in 2002. He currently lives, paints, and works in Ventura California.
